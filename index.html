About me

Hi, I'm Michelle! Nice to meet you.


I'm a recent graduate of Case Western Reserve University with a degree in Computer Science, but now I live in the Seattle area, which is quite an adventure for someone born and raised in rural Michigan. As far as my career goes, I'm a software engineer at Microsoft working in Windows on the Buses Core Team, specifically on USB Type-C (In case you're not familar, "Bus" puts the B in USB).

Evenings and weekends, you can maybe find me working on a side project such as Moonlight. I'm largely responsible for the WinRT client for Windows and Windows Phone. You can read about it more in the section about my projects, but basically it allows you to stream and play Steam games on your mobile device. Cool, right?

I absolutely love to travel. I'm fascinated with this planet and all of its natural beauty and the culture we've built around it. While my career is tech-centric and I have many goals there, my personal life goal is to see the U.S. and the world. I have a travel blog detailing a few of my experiences thus far, and I hope I have the opportunity to fill it with more adventures.

Other "fun" facts about me: I love math (especially cryptography and abstract algebra), coffee, trying (and sometimes failing) to cook yummy things, running, and foreign languages. I'm quite passionate about women in technology. My vice is stupid reality TV shows (The Bachelor, anyone?). I have sound-to-color synesthesia. Mrbit is my goofy nickname (my initials are MRB), hence the site name. Is any of that "fun"?

My Projects

  • Moonlight An open-source version of NVIDIA Gamestream, formerly titled Limelight. Stream Steam games to your other devices and play them from there! In particular, I make the WinRT client for Windows and Windows Phone.

  • PerfectHash Library for Windows written in C to generate a hash function with no collisions. There are no performant implementations of a perfect hash generator that I could find, so I built one.

  • Secrets, Spies and Ciphers: Cryptography in Past, Present, and Future On 1/29/2014, I presented this talk to CWRU Hacker Society about understanding the colorful history of encryption and how it got us where we are today with public key cryptography.

  • RTSPlib Nothing like getting back into the swing of C by writing an RTSP parser! RTSPlib was later integrated into the backend of Limelight, where its bugfixes and feature additions now live.

  • Matrix Actions My final math research paper at school - it's about the crazy things that can happen once you start letting matrices act on polynomials, backed up with some Matlab scripts to help me out. This research project was cool because it's off the beaten path in mathematics and not a well defined subject.

My main areas of interest are driver development and operating systems.
