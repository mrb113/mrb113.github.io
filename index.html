About me

Hi, I'm Michelle! Nice to meet you. I've always found summarizing myself in a few words troublesome, but here's my best shot.


I'm a recent graduate of Case Western Reserve University with a degree in Computer Science, but now I live in the Seattle area. However, my heart is in rural Michigan, where I was born and raised and still consider 'home'. As far as my career goes, I'm a software engineer at Microsoft working in Windows on the next version of the Hardware Certification Kit.

Evenings and weekends, you can often find me working on my side project, Limelight. I'm largely responsible for the client for Windows and Windows Phone. You can read about it more in the section about my projects, but basically it allows you to stream and play Steam games on your mobile device. Cool, right? I'm lucky to be working with such fantastic teammates and friends to bring Limelight to multiple platforms.

Outside of tech-related things, I absolutely love to travel. I'm fascinated with this planet and all of its natural beauty and the culture we've built around it. While my career is tech-centric and I have many goals there, my personal life goal is to see the U.S. and the world. I have a travel blog detailing a few of my experiences thus far, and I hope I'm lucky enough to have the opportunity to fill it with more adventures.

Other "fun" facts about me (I've never equated the feeling I have reading a fact with having fun, but maybe you'll like these anyway): I love math (especially cryptography and abstract algebra), coffee, trying (and sometimes failing) to cook yummy things, outer space, and foreign languages. My vice is stupid reality TV shows (The Bachelor, anyone?). I have sound-to-color synesthesia. Mrbit is my goofy nickname (my initials are MRB), hence the site name. Is any of that "fun"?

My Projects

  • Limelight An open-source version of NVIDIA Gamestream. Stream Steam games to your other devices and play them from there! In particular, I make the WinRT client for Windows and Windows Phone.

  • RTSPlib Nothing like getting back into the swing of C like writing an RTSP parser! RTSPlib was later integrated into the backend of Limelight, where its bugfixes and feature additions now live.

  • Matrix Actions A research paper about the crazy things that can happen once you start letting matrices act on polynomials, backed up with some Matlab scripts to help me out. This research project was cool because it's off the beaten path in mathematics and not a well defined subject.

  • Project Euler From time to time I'm moved to solve some math problems with my computer. Progress here, selected solutions here.

In the future, I'd like to go more down the path of things like driver development and operating systems. However, I'm slowly learning the ropes of CSS and other web things through messing around with website templates like this one!
